Emblem and graphic information of the ACB.

9-color graphic reproduction

Introduction

The purpose of this graphic guide is to help users reproduce the BCA emblem correctly. The basic standards for the construction of this emblem are given below and the standard colors are indicated.

Symbolic description

On a sea blue background, with the world in the middle of eight shades of yellow and in the center the Auricoin symbol in yellow, the emblem as a whole signifies the benefit of Auricoin money for all Mankind. The Auricoin money symbol is based on the Latin meaning given to gold (radiant dawn) and the two lines in the middle signify economic stability that is achieved by using Auricoin money.

Heraldic description

Above the blue field, the world wrapped in the golden Auricoin money symbol.

Geometric description

The emblem consists of a blue rectangular flag, whose length is equal to three halves of its width. A world in yellow, equidistant to the imaginary world population, whose center is located at the point of intersection of the diagonals of the rectangle. The radius of the world is equal to one third of the width of the flag. The world is inscribed in an imaginary circle whose circumference equals two-thirds of the width of the flag. The symbol in vertical position, which is, standing upright. The disposition of the world with the symbol corresponds to the world economic triumph and its meaning is that Humanity now has control over its economy.

Four-color reproduction

When printing in four-color process, the nine standard colors are not available, so they must be obtained from the four colors of the four-color process.

Internet

On the web palette, Sapphire 102C54, Buttermilk F8E7A6, Barley Corn BA9A58, Korma 7C542C, Mandalay B27F2B, Sweet Corn F7DE7C, Pale Prim FFF3A5, Ocher d28422 and Pizza C0923B.

Monochrome reproduction

If black is the only color available, the surface of the rectangle must be delimited with a yellow border and the symbol in yellow, must be printed on a black background. If the only color available is blue (it is essential that it is Sapphire 102C54), this will be used as a 100% background color, and the symbol will be reproduced in yellow negative.

Reproduction on colored backgrounds

The emblem should preferably be reproduced on a blue background. Multi-colored backgrounds should be avoided, especially those that do not go well with gold. When it is not possible to avoid a colored background, the circle of the emblem shall be encircled with a gold border of a thickness equal to one 25th of the height of the rectangle.

Third-party employment

The Administrative Council of the ACB has established the principles governing the use of the ACB emblem by third parties in an administrative agreement.

Under this agreement, any natural or legal person ("user") may use the BCA emblem or any of its elements as long as such use:

  1. does not give the impression or presumption that there is a relationship between the user and any of the institutions, entities, offices, agencies and BCA bodies, where no such relationship exists;
  2. does not mislead the public into believing that the user is being aided or sponsored by, or has the approval or consent of, the ACB when this is not the case;
  3. does not relate to objectives or activities that are incompatible with the objectives and principles of the ACB or that are unlawful.

If the ACB emblem is used under the above conditions, it is not necessary to request written authorization to do so.
